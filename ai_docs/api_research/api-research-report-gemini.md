## **Limitations and Gaps**

1. **Exact Pricing for CurrencyBeacon, Alpha Vantage, and Finnhub:** Despite targeted searches, direct, explicit pricing pages with clear monthly costs for paid tiers (especially those including time series data) were not found for CurrencyBeacon, Alpha Vantage, or Finnhub. Assumptions were made for CurrencyBeacon based on competitor pricing. For Alpha Vantage and Finnhub, while free tier limits are known, the exact costs for higher tiers remain a gap. This makes a precise cost-benefit analysis challenging for these APIs.
2. **Concurrency/Rate Limit Details:** While request limits per day/minute are often stated, detailed information on concurrent requests or burst limits is not always explicitly clear in the publicly available documentation for all APIs.
3. **Specific Time Series Data Granularity:** While "time series" is confirmed, the exact granularities offered (e.g., daily, hourly, minute) and the depth of historical data available through time series for each tier were not exhaustively detailed for every API due to the scope of initial research.
4. **Pay-per-use Models:** The primary pricing model for most APIs appears to be subscription-based. While the prompt preferred pay-per-use, explicit pay-per-use options (beyond just request quotas within a subscription) were not prominently advertised or easily discoverable for all listed APIs.
5. **Long-term Reliability/Uptime:** This research did not include real-world testing or analysis of API uptime, reliability, or latency, which are crucial for a production application.
6. **Community Support and SDKs:** While "ease of use" was assessed qualitatively, a deeper dive into community forums, availability of official SDKs in various languages, and developer support channels was not performed for every API.

## **Recommendations for Action/Further Research**

1. **Prioritize APIs with Clear Pricing:** Focus initial development efforts on APIs that have transparent and easily accessible pricing for their paid tiers, especially those including time series data (e.g., Open Exchange Rates, Fixer/Exchange Rates API). This will enable a more accurate budget forecast.
2. **Contact API Providers for Missing Pricing:** For CurrencyBeacon, Alpha Vantage, and Finnhub, direct contact with their sales or support teams is recommended to obtain precise pricing for plans that include the required features, particularly time series.
3. **Pilot Program for Top Contenders:** Select 2-3 top APIs based on features and *known* pricing (or reasonable estimates). Implement small proof-of-concept integrations to evaluate actual ease of use, response times, data accuracy, and the clarity of their documentation in a real development environment.
4. **Evaluate Free Tier for Initial Development:** Leverage the free tiers of APIs like Alpha Vantage and Finnhub for initial development and testing, given their generous inclusion of historical and time series data, despite the strict rate limits. This can save costs during the early stages.
5. **Consider Combined Approach:** For very specific needs or to mitigate risks, explore if a combination of APIs (e.g., one for live rates, another for deep historical time series) could be more cost-effective or robust, although this adds integration complexity.
6. **Future Research into Uptime/Latency:** Once a few APIs are shortlisted, conduct further research or testing to assess their real-world performance, uptime guarantees, and latency, which are critical for a responsive currency converter app.
7. **Review API Terms of Service:** Carefully review the Terms of Service for each shortlisted API, especially concerning data redistribution, commercial use, and any potential legal implications for the currency converter app.
